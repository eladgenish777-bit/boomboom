# The Amoral Ego Thesis

**Author:** Elad  
**Status:** Draft v0.1  
**Date:** 2026-04-06

## Abstract

This note advances a minimal claim: the ego is not a moral faculty but an amoral action-driving function. It maps motivational pressure into candidate action without itself containing moral categories. Moral judgment emerges only when cognition becomes capable of recursively representing possible action, modeling consequences, and evaluating coherence under a governing criterion. The point is not to deny intuition, habit, or social conditioning, but to deny that their immediacy grants moral authority.

## 1. Primitive Terms

Let:

- $D$ = drive-state
- $A$ = action tendency
- $C$ = consequence-space
- $J$ = evaluative judgment
- $V$ = evaluative criterion
- $V_m$ = moral criterion
- $R$ = recursive reasoning-function
- $E$ = ego-function
- $M$ = moral judgment-function
- $I$ = intuition

## 2. Core Structure

The ego-function is defined as:

$$
E : D \to A
$$

The ego may be shaped by prior learning, habituation, and conditioning, but it does not itself perform explicit recursive evaluation over action-consequence pairs under a criterion. It is therefore not consequence-empty, but non-recursive.

Recursive evaluation is defined as:

$$
R : A \times C \to J
$$

where:

$$
J = R(A, C \mid V)
$$

Not all judgment is moral. Moral judgment is the special case where recursive evaluation operates under a specifically moral criterion:

$$
M = R(A, C \mid V_m)
$$

## 3. Main Thesis

The ego generates action without morality. Moral judgment is not native to the ego-layer. It appears only when cognition recursively evaluates candidate actions and projected consequences under a moral criterion.

So:

- ego initiates movement,
- recursive cognition evaluates,
- morality is a special case of evaluation rather than a primitive drive.

## 4. Consequences

### 4.1 Pre-moral action

Any action tendency produced by $E$ alone is pre-moral. It may later be judged moral or immoral, but its production is not itself moral judgment.

### 4.2 Motivational conflict is not moral judgment

Competing drives, urges, or pressures are not yet morality. Inner conflict explains tension among candidate actions, but not how one candidate becomes justified, forbidden, or coherent under judgment.

### 4.3 Intuition is not verdict

An intuition $I$ may be fast, affectively intense, and action-guiding, but these features do not make it morally authoritative. Intuition enters reasoning as candidate material. It is data, not verdict.

## 5. Automaticity Does Not Ground

Let $\tau(x)$ denote automaticity and $\phi(x)$ denote grounding. Then:

$$
\tau(x) \not\Rightarrow \phi(x)
$$

A response may be rapid, fluent, and deeply habituated without thereby becoming morally justified. Automaticity is evidence of consolidation, not authority.

## 6. One-Line Statement

**The ego moves. Recursive reason loops. Moral judgment emerges only when possible action is evaluated under projected consequence and a governing moral criterion.**

## 7. Scope

This note does not yet specify the final content of $V_m$. Its claim is structural: it specifies the conditions under which moral judgment becomes possible, not a complete moral theory.
